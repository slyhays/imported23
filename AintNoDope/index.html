Economics (3)

Where do the tokens come from?
The DOPE network continually creates new tokens to reward content creators and curators. Some of the newly-created tokens are transferred to users who add value to DOPE by posting, commenting, and voting on other people's posts. The remainder is distributed to witnesses that power the blockchain.
How are new DOPE coins created? How are they distributed?
Blockchains like DOPE and Bitcoin produce new tokens each time a block is created. Unlike Bitcoin, where all of the new coins go to the block producers (called miners), the DOPE blockchain allocates a majority of the new tokens to a reward fund called the "rewards pool". The rewards pool gives users tokens for participating in the platform based on the value they add.

Out of the new tokens that are generated: 80% go to the reward pool, which is split between authors and curators. The remaining 20% oays for the witnesses to power the blockchain.
Where does the value come from?
At its root, DOPE is simply a point system. However, because this point system is blockchain-based, the points can be traded on the market as tokens. People but and sell these tokens, and many hold in anticipation of increased purchasing power for various DOPE relatied services.

By analogy, DOPE is a game system where users compete for attention and rewards by bringing content and adding value to the platform. The rewards people earn are tokens that have market value and are readily tradable.
It is similar to how someone playing a video game could obtain a limited item or currency by playing the game. If the currency or items are transferable between users, then they can sell or buy them on game item markets.

Decentralization (4)

What is Decentralization?
Transactions are stored on a blockchain in a decentralized network. This means no one entity ever holds all the data. Instead, data is spread between nodes, or connection points, within the network. This structure mkaes it hard for a network to be overruled and controlled by an attacker and allows for a more transparent, fair and secure network.
How does a decentralized social media platform work?
The most important thing to note about decentralized social media is that it doesn't consist of one central server. Many of the biggest social media networks today, including Meta(Facebook), Twitter, and TikTok, are all centralized, meaning one core authorityu holds all the power over the network. This can be dangerous, as it increased the risk of large scale hacks, takeovers and data leaks (ie Cambridge Associates FB Scandal).

Decentralized social media outlets have a very special characteristics: multiple distributed networks can exist within them. This means that individuals can choose which networks they'd like to join. Any user can create a network of their own, allowing for individuals with similar interests or ideas to connect and create together.

Users can vote to have their say in how the platform progresses, instead of having to go along with whatever the central authority wants.

Decentralized social media outlets offer a fresh take on digital communitcaion, wherein multiple networks can exist within one platform, all of which are controlled by independent servers.
What are the benefits of a decentralized social media site?
Increased Security
You may have heard of popular social media networks malfunctioning in the past because the server crashed; the chance of a total network failure due to technical error is massively reduced on a decentralized network by using multiple, independent servers to function, unlike traditional platforms. Also, users don't need to traditionally identify themselves on decentralized social media networks. They can choose to go by pseudonyms, giving them an added layer of privacy.

Increased User Control One thing users just dont have enough of when using social media platforms is control. A social media network is defined by its users and develops and accordance with user demand. So, it makes sense that the users themselves should harbor some kind of control within the social network they're using. This is one of the core concepts behind decentralized social media. Giving users control of their data, interactions, and social media experience is key to providing a fair and equal network for all.
What are the drawbacks of a decentralized social media site?
There's no doubt that decentralized social media platforms offer some exciting perks but there are some risks associated with this new technology. The biggest threat is that malicious individuals can take advantage of how it's structured. Free speech and the idea of creating multiple, decentralized networks sounds pretty cool, but there's always a chance that hate speech, propaganda, and illegal content could quickly spread through the creation of harmful communities. Because you can post what you want within your network, it becomes much easier for malicious groups to spread their beliefs publicly.

Many decentralized social media platforms have an ethical code of conduct in place in an attempt to stomp out hate.

Miscellaneous (3)

What is a blockchain?
A blockchain is a public ledger of all transactions ever executed. All of the transactions and data are stored in a database. Each time the database is updated, all of updates are done together in a batch called a 'block'. Each time a new block is produced/added, it is appended on to all of the previous blocks - hence the name "blockchain".
What is the DOPE blockchain?
The DOPE blockchain is the publicly accessible distributed database, which records all posts and votes, and distributes the rewards across the network. It is where all of the text content and voting data is stored, and it is where all of the reward calculations and payouts are performed.
